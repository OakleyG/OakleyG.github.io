Physics Lab

Brownian Motion and Diffusion Coefficient of Microspheres

Analyzed the Brownian motion of microspheres suspended in liquid using digital video microscopy — using mean squared displacement, weighted linear fits and the Stokes-Einstein relation to confirm the stochastic nature of thermal motion and highlight the importance of accurate uncertainty estimation in physical measurements.

Physics Lab

Velocity of Ultrasonic Waves in Water

The Debye-Sears effect was employed to measure the speed of sound in water and determine its bulk modulus. The experiment utilised optical diffraction from an acoustically driven standing wave in a liquid medium, with angular deflection calibrated using a sodium lamp and a diffraction grating.
